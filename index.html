The Moment

Karnataka is about to see its biggest wave of frontline jobs in a generation.

Two years of investment have built a huge jobs pipeline. Most of it is frontline work: EV and factory lines, data centres, construction, warehousing, retail and care.

One question decides everything. Will these jobs go to Kannadiga youth, skilled and placed at home? Or to workers brought in from outside, because ours weren't ready in time?

The Readiness Gap

These jobs can — and should — go to Kannadigas first.

Right now, many of these jobs go to workers from other states. Officially 46,393 are registered; the real number is far higher, and 88% are in construction. That's not a threat. It's demand our own youth aren't ready to fill yet.

Close that gap, and the same jobs go to skilled Kannadigas. The wages, taxes and skills stay in Karnataka. The goal isn't to shut anyone out. It's to make our own youth the first choice, in every district, not just Bengaluru.

The youth of North & Kalyana Karnataka are ready to work. What's missing is the pipeline that connects them to the jobs.

The System's Gap  ·  CAG audit, 2025

Built to train. Not to place.

The state auditor (CAG, 2025) reviewed six years of skilling, from 2017 to 2023, across successive governments. The problem was never effort. It was design: training that ran ahead of real jobs.

→Courses were picked without checking employer vacancies. No skill-gap study came first.
→Training reached only 170 of 719 job roles.
→The result: certificates that didn't become jobs.
We flip the order: start with the job, then skill, then verify the placement.

Sources & Notes

The evidence behind this proposal.

  • Invest Karnataka 2025 — ₹10.27 lakh Cr committed, 6 lakh jobs projected; ~2.3 lakh realised by end-2025 (Govt. of Karnataka).
  • Invest Karnataka Conclave, Feb 2026 — ₹51,469 Cr / 70,000+ jobs; sector split across ESDM & data centres, aerospace, manufacturing, auto/EV.
  • KA Labour Department, 2026 — 46,393 registered inter-state migrants (a floor); 40,945 (88%) in construction; top-5 source states 72.5%.
  • Quick-commerce hiring data, 2025 — Karnataka #1 hub at 20% of national demand.
  • CAG Karnataka, Report No. 6 of 2025 (CMKKY) — 18.18% placement vs 70% target; training in 170 of 719 roles; courses chosen without demand evidence; northern districts held zero skill-mission meetings 2017–23.
  • PLFS 2023–24 (MoSPI) — Karnataka female LFPR ~38%; occupational segregation across frontline sectors.
  • Garment sector, 2025 — ~5 lakh workers in Bengaluru, ~90% women; substantial inter-district mobility.
  • Karnataka Clean Mobility Policy 2025–30 — ₹50,000 Cr / 1 lakh jobs by 2030. GCC — 3.5 lakh jobs by 2029. Tourism Policy 2024–29 — 47,000 direct + 1 lakh+ indirect.
  • Karnataka Platform-Based Gig Workers (Social Security & Welfare) Act 2025 — 1–5% welfare fee & state board.
  • BetterPlace company figures — 30M+ workers, 1,500+ enterprises, 500+ cities; 2.5M+ skilled, 2.5L+ placed; IFFCO Tokio 70%→89%, 4 AI agents, 50k+ advisors.

Investment & policy job figures are Government of Karnataka commitments/targets and must not be summed. Migrant counts are registration floors. Year-3 outcomes are targets. BetterPlace figures are company-reported.
